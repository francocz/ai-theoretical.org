Abstract

The dominant narrative of contemporary political economy holds that advanced economies have experienced decades of stagnating real wages and rising inequality, with the gains from technological progress accruing to a shrinking elite. This paper argues that this narrative rests on a statistical measurement illusion. Conventional national-accounts and consumer-price methodologies — designed for a mid-twentieth-century economy of standardized physical goods — systematically fail to capture the value generated by quality improvements, dematerialization, and the creation of entirely new goods and services.
